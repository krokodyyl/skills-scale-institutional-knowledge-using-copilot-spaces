# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead / Tester

### Role Summary
The QA Lead / Tester is responsible for ensuring that software meets defined quality standards before release. They coordinate testing activities, report defects, and sign off on quality gates throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Create, maintain, and execute test cases and test plans
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Report and track defects through to resolution
- Sign off on quality gates before release
- Maintain the Definition of Done and acceptance criteria validation

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Ensure all acceptance criteria are verified before release
- Improve test coverage and testing efficiency over time

### Typical Communication
- Collaborates with Developers on test design and testability
- Works with Product Managers to validate acceptance criteria
- Coordinates with Project Managers on release readiness and quality status

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure the product is usable, intuitive, and aligned with user needs. They conduct research, create prototypes and design specifications, and collaborate across teams to deliver a high-quality user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability studies
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity design specifications
- Hand off design specs to Developers for implementation
- Gather and incorporate user feedback post-launch
- Maintain design consistency across the product

### Goals
- Deliver designs that solve real user problems
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Ensure consistent, accessible user experiences

### Typical Communication
- Works closely with Product Managers during requirements gathering and prioritization
- Supports Developers during implementation with design clarifications
- Helps QA understand the intended user experience for acceptance testing

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
The Security Champion promotes secure development practices within the team and serves as the primary point of contact for security-related activities, reviews, and incident response support.

### Responsibilities
- Identify security risks early in the development lifecycle
- Promote and enforce secure coding practices
- Conduct or coordinate security reviews and threat modeling
- Support security incident response and post-incident reviews
- Stay current on relevant vulnerabilities and compliance requirements

### Goals
- Reduce the risk of security incidents and vulnerabilities
- Embed security awareness into day-to-day development
- Ensure compliance with relevant security standards

### Typical Communication
- Advises Developers and QA on vulnerabilities and secure practices
- Coordinates with Project Managers on compliance requirements and timelines
- Escalates significant security issues to Sponsors or leadership when necessary

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, pipelines, and automation that enable reliable and repeatable software delivery. They bridge development and operations to support continuous integration and deployment.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage cloud infrastructure and environment configurations
- Execute and oversee production deployments
- Respond to operational incidents and monitor system health
- Collaborate on infrastructure-as-code and capacity planning

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize deployment risk and recovery time
- Maintain high system availability and observability

### Typical Communication
- Collaborates with Developers on environment setup, configuration, and tooling
- Keeps Project Managers updated on deployment status and operational incidents
- Supports QA with provisioning and maintaining test environments

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide strategic direction, allocate resources, and serve as key decision-makers at critical project milestones. They are responsible for ensuring the project aligns with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide strategic direction and business context for the project
- Allocate budget and resources as needed
- Review project progress at key milestones and gates
- Make go/no-go decisions at release or phase boundaries
- Resolve escalated blockers that require executive action

### Goals
- Ensure project outcomes align with business objectives
- Enable the team to deliver by removing organizational blockers
- Maintain visibility into project risk and progress

### Typical Communication
- Consulted by Project Managers and Product Managers for approvals and direction
- Receives regular status updates (weekly or milestone-based)
- May intervene directly to resolve escalated blockers or reprioritize scope
